More About Me

I'm a software developer with a background in Civil Engineering and
a passion for solving the difficult engineering problems of the modern world. In my
free time, I enjoy gaming, photography, cycling, and hiking all around California.

My Story

I’ve always been a bit of an engineer. When I was two years old, I began taking things apart just to see how they worked. Later, when I was ten, I taught myself a bit of programming and built my first website, then won an award in high school for a website depicting a historical house in San Ramon. I loved math and took college-level programming classes while still in high school.

I received a bachelors degree, and then a masters degree in civil and environmental engineering from UC Davis, specializing in geotechnical engineering. While working on my thesis, I improved the performance of constitutive models using C++, cementing my love affair with coding.

Over the next two years, I worked as a civil engineer by day, and took various online classes, teaching myself programming by night. Now I’d like to expand my horizons by combining my skills in unique ways that will benefit society, perhaps contributing to the internet of things. I am fluent in several languages and frameworks, including React.js/Flux, JavaScript, Ruby, Ruby on Rails, jQuery, PostgreSQL, and C++, know my way around RSpec, CSS3/HTML5 and GIT, and I’ve dabbled in Scalla, Machine Learning, and Android programming.
